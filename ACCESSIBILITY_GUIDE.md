# ARound Navigator - Accessibility User Guide

## Quick Start for Screen Reader Users

This app is designed specifically for blind and visually impaired users. All features are accessible via voice commands and have screen reader support.

## Voice Commands Reference

### Available Commands

1. **"What do you see?"**
   - Tells you what objects are detected
   - Example response: "I can see 1 chair, 2 persons, 1 table"

2. **"Describe scene"**
   - Gives detailed information about each object
   - Includes position and distance
   - Example: "Chair very close on left. Person medium distance on right."

3. **"Pause speech"**
   - Stops automatic navigation announcements
   - Use when you need quiet

4. **"Resume" or "Continue"**
   - Restarts navigation announcements

5. **"Scan"**
   - Manually checks the environment
   - Same as pressing Scan button

6. **"Help"**
   - Lists all available voice commands

## Navigation Guidance

### Understanding Position
- **"On your left"** - Object is to the left side
- **"In front"** - Object is directly ahead
- **"On your right"** - Object is to the right side

### Understanding Distance
- **"Very close"** - Less than 1 meter away - STOP
- **"Close"** - About 1-2 meters away - CAUTION
- **"Ahead"** - 2-4 meters away - BE AWARE
- **"No distance mention"** - Far away, not immediately dangerous

### Danger Warnings
- **"Warning!"** prefix means STOP immediately
- You'll also feel strong vibration
- Listen for the navigation suggestion that follows

### Navigation Suggestions
- **"Move right"** - Obstacle on your left
- **"Move left"** - Obstacle on your right
- **"Stop or move aside"** - Obstacle directly in front

## Haptic Feedback (Vibration)

The app uses vibration to warn you:

1. **Strong rapid vibration** (200ms-100ms-200ms)
   - Very dangerous obstacle
   - Very close to you
   - STOP IMMEDIATELY

2. **Medium vibration** (150ms-100ms-150ms)
   - Dangerous obstacle
   - Close to you
   - USE CAUTION

3. **Light vibration** (100ms)
   - Moderate concern
   - Object detected ahead

4. **No vibration**
   - Objects are far away
   - No immediate danger

## Button Controls

### Bottom Control Panel (from left to right)

1. **Navigation Toggle** (default: ON)
   - Left side switch
   - When ON: Automatically announces obstacles
   - When OFF: Silent mode, use manual buttons

2. **Voice Commands Toggle** (default: OFF)
   - Right side switch
   - When ON: App listens for voice commands
   - When OFF: Voice commands disabled

3. **Scan Button**
   - First large button
   - Press to hear what objects are detected
   - Use when you need to check surroundings

4. **Describe Button**
   - Second large button
   - Press for detailed scene description
   - Includes positions and distances

5. **GPU Acceleration**
   - Bottom switch
   - Keep ON for faster detection
   - Turn OFF if phone gets too hot

## Screen Reader Support

### With TalkBack/Screen Reader

All buttons have descriptive labels:
- "Toggle navigation guidance"
- "Toggle voice commands"
- "Scan environment"
- "Describe current scene"

The app is fully usable with TalkBack and other screen readers.

## Tips for Best Experience

### For Optimal Detection
1. **Hold Phone Vertically**
   - Camera should face forward
   - Point in direction you're walking

2. **Steady Movements**
   - Move phone slowly
   - Avoid rapid movements
   - Give app time to detect

3. **Good Lighting**
   - Works best in daylight or well-lit rooms
   - May struggle in very dark areas

4. **Distance from Objects**
   - Detection works best at 0.5 to 5 meters
   - Too close: May miss objects
   - Too far: May not detect smaller items

### For Best Audio Experience
1. **Volume Level**
   - Set to comfortable level
   - Should hear clearly but not too loud

2. **In Quiet Environments**
   - Better speech recognition
   - Clearer obstacle warnings

3. **Use Headphones**
   - More private
   - Better audio quality
   - Less distracting to others

### Battery Life
1. **GPU Acceleration**
   - Faster but uses more battery
   - Turn off for longer sessions

2. **Brightness**
   - Lower screen brightness
   - Camera preview doesn't need bright screen

3. **Close Other Apps**
   - Better performance
   - Longer battery life

## Safety Guidelines

### ⚠️ IMPORTANT SAFETY INFORMATION

1. **This is an ASSISTANCE tool, not a replacement for:**
   - White cane
   - Guide dog
   - Human assistance
   - Your own judgment

2. **Limitations:**
   - May not detect all obstacles
   - May miss small objects on ground
   - May not work well in darkness
   - Requires phone battery
   - Depends on camera view

3. **Always:**
   - Use with your primary mobility aid
   - Be cautious in unfamiliar areas
   - Listen to your surroundings
   - Ask for help when needed

4. **Never:**
   - Rely solely on this app
   - Use while crossing streets
   - Use in hazardous environments
   - Use with low battery

## Troubleshooting

### Speech Not Working
1. Check volume is not muted
2. Try toggle Navigation OFF then ON
3. Restart the app
4. Check system Text-to-Speech settings

### Voice Commands Not Responding
1. Make sure "Voice Cmd" toggle is ON
2. Check microphone permission is granted
3. Speak clearly and at normal volume
4. Reduce background noise
5. Try saying "Help" to test

### No Vibration
1. Check phone is not in silent mode
2. Check "Do Not Disturb" is off
3. Some phones don't support vibration
4. Check phone settings

### Camera Not Working
1. Grant camera permission
2. Close other apps using camera
3. Restart the app
4. Restart your phone

### App is Slow
1. Turn ON GPU Acceleration
2. Close background apps
3. Restart the app
4. Check available phone storage

## Privacy & Permissions

### Why Permissions Are Needed

1. **Camera**
   - To see and detect obstacles
   - Required for app to work

2. **Microphone (Record Audio)**
   - Only for voice commands
   - Optional - can use without it
   - Not recorded or saved

3. **Vibrate**
   - For haptic warnings
   - Optional but recommended

### Your Privacy
- Everything stays on your phone
- No internet connection needed
- No data sent anywhere
- No recording of audio or video
- No tracking

## Getting Help

### Voice Help
Say "Help" to hear available commands anytime.

### Test the App Safely
1. Ask someone to help first time
2. Test in a safe, familiar space
3. Learn the voice commands
4. Practice with objects you know

### Emergency
If you need urgent help, use your phone's emergency features or ask nearby people for assistance.
